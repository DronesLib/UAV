Video Description
It should be noted that real LiDAR systems typically operate in the infrared spectrum, which is not visible to the human eye. In this simulation, the laser beams are visualized to provide a clearer illustration of the sensing process. Specifically, beams that do not intersect with obstacles are shown in green, while those that hit obstacles are displayed in red. It is important to emphasize that, in real-world LiDAR systems, the wavelength (i.e., color) of the laser does not change upon detecting an obstacle.

Furthermore, visualizing all laser beams at the full LiDAR resolution can significantly reduce simulation performance and lead to noticeable lag. To ensure a smoother viewing experience, a sparse visualization strategy is adopted. It should be clarified that this sparsity only applies to the visualization, while the full-resolution LiDAR data are still used in the actual experiments.

Two video files are provided for demonstration. The file “Autonomous_flightLiDAR_show.mp4” presents the complete autonomous flight process, including the LiDAR visualization and the UAV trajectory. To improve playback smoothness and avoid rendering delays caused by dense laser beams, the file “Autonomous_flight.mp4” omits the visualization of the laser beams. It should be noted that the LiDAR sensor itself is not removed; only its visual representation is disabled.
